When The Sea Glows

My Perspective on Bioluminescence in Wemmbu and Eggchan's Loose Guidebook on How to Visit the Beach by Ydmnnn on AO3

What Makes the Sea Glow?

Bioluminescence seas occur when microscopic organism called dinoflagellates emit light when disturbed, turning ocean waves into something that looks almost magical. It's not a regular occurence–which makes stumbling upon it feel like something that can't be planned, only found by accident, by the right people at the right time.

In the Fanfic

In chapter 4, Wemmbu sees a glowing sea for the first time—small luminous particles following the waves, almost mirroring the stars above them.

"Something sparkling caught the demon's eyes. Wemmbu could see the sea now, it was glowing... It almost matched the sky with its stars, gently twinkling above them like they were watching over the teenagers."

And in his usual, unbothered way, he asks Egg only one thing:

"Hey Egg, why is the sea glowing?"

Because he knew Egg would have the answer.

What It Means

Bioluminescence only appears under certain conditions—fragile, fleeting, impossible to force. Much like that night itself. Much like Egg. And Wemmbu, who rarely stops for anything, paused to marvel at it–because Egg was there beside him.

It was beautifully peaceful.

That line belongs to Wemmbu. And it says everything.

A Reader's Note

This fanfic is something I will carry with me forever. What started as a story about two best friends sneaking out to the beach ended up changing the way I see the world—quiet literally.

I never thought much about bioluminescence seas before. But after reading this, I find myself looking at them differently–as something genuinely breathtaking, worth every mile traveled to witness. A place quiet enough for reflection, and rare enough to feel like a gift.

But what makes the glowing sea in this fanfic feel truly magical is Wemmbu and Eggchan standing in front of it together. Their friendship—loud and quiet all at once, sturdy yet fragile–makes the moment feel bigger than it is. The sea glows, and somehow, so does everything around them.

And somehow, so does this story.
